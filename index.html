🐔 What Makes Sonali Eggs Special?

Sonali is a premium country-chicken breed — a cross between the Rhode Island Red rooster and the Fayoumi hen — famous across India and Bangladesh for its hardiness and rich, flavourful eggs.

🐓

Sonali — the golden bird of the countryside

🌾 Naturally Hardy Breed

Sonali birds are naturally disease-resistant and thrive in open, free-range farm conditions — so they need far fewer medicines than commercial broiler farms use.

🥚 Richer, Tastier Eggs

Sonali hens lay light-brown eggs with a deep golden-orange yolk, a firmer white, and a rich "desi" taste you simply don't get from white poultry eggs.

💛 Closer to Pure Natu Kodi

Sonali eggs carry the nutrition profile of traditional country eggs — more flavour, denser nutrients, and a natural diet of grains, greens and free-range foraging.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you want to know before ordering

How fresh are the eggs?
Eggs are collected from the farm the same day or a day before delivery. We never use cold storage — you get them the way nature intended.
How long do country eggs stay good?
Kept in a cool, dry place they stay fresh for about 2 weeks; refrigerated, up to 3–4 weeks. Country eggs have stronger shells, which helps them keep longer.
Why are natu kodi eggs costlier than white eggs?
Free-range Sonali hens lay fewer eggs than caged commercial birds, and they're raised on natural feed without growth shortcuts. You pay a little more for much better taste and nutrition.
Do you sell country chicken also?
